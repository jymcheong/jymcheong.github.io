    Attack Life Cycle

    Attackers RINSE & repeat...

    • Reconnaissance - Gather information to assess value & vulnerabilities aka System Susceptibility
    • Infiltrate Networks - Get into target networks aka Threat Accessibility
    • Spread Laterally - Stay within target networks aka Threat Capability to stay undetected
    • Execute Objectives - Results to compromised Confidentiality (stolen), Integrity (tampered) & Availability (denied access) of data & networks. For Cyber Physical systems, it can lead to life threatening scenarios.

    Direct vs Indirect Access to Objectives

    Directly from 1.Recon to 4.Execute Objectives -> Poor web-server configuration leads to exposure of unprotected sensitive files that can be downloaded by anyone. Can't even consider this as an attack, low hanging fruits for adversaries!

    From 1.Recon to 2.Infiltrate Networks -> Attackers use LinkedIn to figure out who are the HR executives, send deceptive emails to lure them (Phishing) to click on malicious links or execute malware. Just need one employee to give attackers that chance to gain control over a HR asset when the malware infects like a Patient Zero & persists within that endpoint. Why go through all that trouble when attackers can buy access into corporate VPN?

    First scenario clear illustrates the 3 conditions, valuable information in the form of files are there left exposed, anyone can explore such web-server directories without being noticed. Attackers just need to scan a range of Internet addresses to find such poorly configured servers.

    Success or failure of cyber-physical attack depends on Necessary and Sufficient prerequisites. Attacks are effective only when all conditions are fulfilled, yet it will fail if any single condition is disrupted. For instance, when employees are vigilant, they report the phishing emails. With robust multi-factor VPN authentication, 1st factor of ID & password sold on dark web is less threatening.

    Disrupt Attack Life Cycle by Design instead of...

    Reacting to yet another vulnerability announcement, another patching exercise (e.g. Microsoft Patch Tuesday) or worst, an automatic update that disrupted millions of hosts. Basically stuck in a Chase-After-Bad posture, which is unending & costly.

    Threat Accessibility is a viable condition that we can disrupt attacks effectively by design to achieve usability & security simultaneously.

    What about the other two conditions? They contribute to our ending chase!

    • Keeping up with vulnerabilities by patching (2018 RDP issue after patch, 2024 July patch broke RDP again) is very challenging & sometimes disastrous (Please DO NOT mistaken this as a DON'T patch advice, but rather the need to be selective & mindful of risks.)
    • Threat actors are extremely motivated, creative & resourceful. They use free or cheap tools to evade costly controls & monitoring operations at a fraction of the cost!

    To go beyond justifying Threat Accessibility as a disruption point in the Attack Life Cycle framework, it's crucial to emphasize the need to steer clear of Tit-for-Tat controls, which may result in an unfavorable Chase-After-Bad position.️

    Replace Tit-for-Tat Controls with Design Thinking

    The Crowdstrike Channel File incident is a prime example of Tit-for-Tat control. Updates are needed to counter whatever new offensive techniques.

    These controls are primarily designed to counteract attack techniques and address vulnerabilities in an operating system or network. Due to the ever-evolving nature of offensive tactics & techniques, continuous updating is essential to keep pace with new threats - thereby classified as reactive or Tit-for-Tat controls.️
